# Constitution — Solo Working

> _"In a world where only the strong survive, I — a rank-F employee — was chosen by the System. Not to save the world. To finish the report by 5 PM."_

---

## 1. Core Values — What This Novel Stands For

### Primary Theme

**Dignity in the grind.** Office work is treated with the same gravity and epic weight as world-ending battles. The joke is never that work is _unimportant_ — the joke is that our protagonist treats every task like it _is_ the final boss.

### Secondary Theme

**The absurdity of hustle culture.** The System rewards productivity, but the System also demands everything. Power comes at a cost — and that cost is overtime.

### Message

_Anyone can become the greatest — not through talent, but through sheer, unrelenting willingness to just... keep working._

---

## 2. Tone & Style — The Sacred Rules

### [Core Requirement] Copy Solo Leveling's Exact Narrative Voice

- Dead serious. No winking at the audience.
- The protagonist NEVER acknowledges the absurdity. The world is dramatic. He lives in it dramatically.
- Internal monologue is frequent, philosophical, and occasionally menacing.
- System notifications appear in formatted boxes (STATUS WINDOW, QUEST ALERT, etc.).
- Descriptions of mundane things (a stapler, a PowerPoint slide, instant noodles) use the same language as epic fantasy combat.

### [Core Requirement] The Parody Lives in the Gap

The humor comes from **juxtaposition** — never from the characters being aware they're funny. A performance review is narrated like a duel to the death. An email chain is described like a labyrinth of deceit. The reader laughs; the protagonist doesn't.

### Sentence Structure

- Short, punchy sentences during tense moments. _Three words. Then impact._
- Longer, flowing sentences during internal reflection, mimicking the cadence of the original.
- Occasional ALL CAPS for system messages and pivotal revelations.

### Atmosphere

- Office environments feel dimly lit, dangerous, and oppressive — even the break room.
- The lighting is always described as _fluorescent and merciless._
- Time pressure is existential. A 5 PM deadline feels like an apocalypse.

---

## 3. Language Principles

### [Core Requirement] Filipino Cultural Flavor — Baked In, Not Sprinkled

- The protagonist thinks in Filipino cadence. Occasional untranslated Tagalog/Filipino lines are acceptable and add authenticity.
- The protagonist has two sacred commands. Both are treated with the gravity of a death sentence:
  - **"Rise."** — the deploy command. Summons Shadow Workers already under his command into active service. The parody of "Arise."
  - **"Raise!"** — the ability command. Permanently converts a broken worker into a Shadow Worker. Every bystander who hears it thinks he is demanding a salary increase. He is not. Or maybe he is. It is never explained.
- Filipino workplace culture is cannon: the hierarchy (Boss → Manager → Senior → Rank-F Employee), the bayanihan of silently covering for a coworker, the art of _pakikisama_.

### [Core Requirement] English is the Primary Language

- The novel is written in English. Filipino phrases are used for flavor, emphasis, and parody punch — never as a crutch.

### Forbidden Phrases

- No "suddenly" unless used ironically in a system notification.
- No explaining the joke. Ever.
- No breaking the fourth wall. The world is real. The stakes are real.

## 4. Character Principles

### The Protagonist — [Name TBD]

- Starts as the weakest employee in the company. Rank F. No skills. No connections. No charm.
- After awakening the System, he becomes methodical, cold, and unnervingly competent.
- He does not celebrate victories loudly. He acknowledges them internally. _Another task cleared._
- He is not cruel, but he is ruthless in completing his objectives.
- His one vanity: he always submits work before the deadline. Always.

### Shadow Workers — The "Raised" Subordinates

- [Core Requirement] Instead of undead shadow soldiers, the protagonist can **Raise** capable workers he has "defeated" (out-performed, helped, or rescued professionally).
- When summoned, they answer with their specialty: _"Database migration, ready."_ — _"Presentation design, standing by."_ — _"The pivot table has been corrected."_
- The command to summon is: **"Rise."**
- The command to create a new Shadow Worker is: **"Raise!"**
- They are fanatically loyal. They do not eat. They do not sleep. They do not ask for raises. (The irony of this is noted. It is not commented on.)

### Supporting Cast Archetypes (to be specified in story spec)

- The Rival — a high-ranked employee who looks down on Rank-F workers
- The Mentor — a senior employee who saw potential and paid for it
- The Guild (Department) — colleagues who oscillate between allies and liabilities
- The Dungeon Master (Executive) — the true final boss of each arc: a C-suite officer with unreasonable demands

## 5. Worldbuilding Principles

### The Work System — Rules That Cannot Change

- The System appeared to the protagonist alone. No one else can see it.
- The System gives QUESTS (tasks), DAILY MISSIONS (recurring work duties), PENALTIES for missed deadlines (stat debuffs — fatigue, brain fog, social anxiety), and REWARDS (stat boosts, new skills, shadow worker slots).
- Stats tracked by the System: **FOCUS**, **STAMINA**, **NETWORKING**, **PRESENTATION**, **DEADLINE SENSE**, **CORPORATE SURVIVAL**
- The System is not kind. It does not explain itself. It only evaluates output.

### The World

- The setting is modern corporate Philippines — a mid-sized company in BGC or Makati.
- The company has an internal ranking system for employees that mirrors the hunter rank system (S, A, B, C, D, E, F).
- "Dungeons" are high-stakes projects: a major client pitch, a system migration, a budget season, an audit.
- "Monsters" are obstacles: a data breach, a passive-aggressive senior, a client who changes requirements at midnight.

### The Power of "Raise"

- The protagonist's unique ability is not strength or speed. It is **Raise** — the ability to take workers who have been "broken" by corporate life (burned out, undervalued, quietly suffering) and restore them as Shadow Workers under his command.
- Each Raised worker retains their original skill set but operates at peak performance, without fatigue, without doubt.
- The protagonist never refers to this as magic or a power. He calls it _delegation._

---

## 6. Quality Non-Negotiables

- [Core Requirement] Every chapter must open with either a system notification OR a one-sentence internal monologue that sets the stakes.
- [Core Requirement] Each "dungeon" (arc) must have a clear entry point, a turning point, and a resolution that advances the protagonist's rank or unlocks a new ability/shadow worker.
- [Core Requirement] **"Rise."** must land like a deployment command from hell — cold, final, irresistible.
- [Core Requirement] **"Raise!"** must always happen at peak dramatic tension. Every nearby character who hears it must visibly react — and their reaction must be about salary. The comedy is in the gap. The power is real.
- The tone never breaks. If the comedy lands, it lands _because_ the tone holds.
- Filipino readers must feel seen. International readers must feel like they've discovered something.
