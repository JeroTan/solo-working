# Chapter 10 — The Midnight Presentation

*The best presentations aren't made of slides. They’re made of the blood, sweat, and tears that you’ve managed to hide behind a clean sans-serif font. And sometimes, they’re made of shadows.*

---

The 11th floor was a graveyard of ambition at 11:43 PM. 

The cleaning crew had already passed through, leaving the scent of lemon-scented chemicals to compete with the stale air. The only light came from the emergency exit signs and a single, flickering cubicle at the far end of the row.

Cynthia Ocampo was still there. 

She was slumped over her keyboard, her face illuminated by the harsh white light of her screen. Her 'Creative Drive' bar, once a vibrant purple, was now a dull, flickering gray. 

```
║  STATUS: CYNTHIA OCAMPO                  ║
║  Breaking Point: 97% [||||||||||]        ║
║  Condition: [ CRITICAL EXHAUSTION ]      ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

She reached for her cold cup of coffee, took a sip, and made a face. Then, she looked at the slide she had been working on for three hours. It was a diagram of the API architecture—the real one, the one Aldrich had told her to ignore.

"It doesn't matter," she whispered to the empty office. "He's just going to delete it anyway."

She moved her mouse to the 'Delete' key.

"Don't."

Cynthia jumped, her chair skidding across the linoleum. She turned, her hand over her heart, and saw Juan dela Cruz standing in the shadows of the next cubicle.

"Juan! You... you scared me. What are you still doing here?"

"I'm a Rank-E now," Juan said, stepping into the light. "I have more responsibilities. And more logs to check."

He walked over and looked at her screen. "The API structure is correct. If you delete it, the client will know we're faking the scalability."

"Aldrich doesn't care about scalability, Juan. He cares about the Ferrari," Cynthia said, her voice cracking. "I've made forty slides of Ferraris. I’m done."

She closed her eyes, and for the first time, Juan saw a tear escape. She was broken. The corporate dungeon had finally claimed its most talented prisoner.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              ⚠ TARGET BROKEN ⚠             ║
║                                          ║
║  CYNTHIA OCAMPO has reached 100%         ║
║  Breaking Point.                         ║
║                                          ║
║  Requirement for [ RAISE ] met.          ║
║  Do you wish to proceed?                 ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan looked at her. He didn't feel pity. He felt a cold, professional recognition. 

"Cynthia," he said, his voice dropping to a low, resonant tone. "Do you want to win this pitch? Not for Aldrich. For yourself."

Cynthia laughed, a jagged, hopeless sound. "How? I can't even keep my eyes open."

"Then let me help."

Juan reached out his hand. He didn't touch her, but his shadow—now a massive, flickering presence that filled the cubicle—reached out and touched her reflection in the dark monitor.

"Raise," Juan whispered.

Cynthia didn't die. She didn't turn into a shadow. But a part of her—the part that had once been the 'Presentation Queen', the part that had been suppressed for years—suddenly detached itself from her slumped body.

A dark, elegant figure emerged from the shadows beneath her desk. It had Cynthia’s silhouette, her sharp features, but its eyes were glowing with a relentless, violet fire.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              SHADOW WORKER READY         ║
║                                          ║
║  CYNTHIA (THE FALLEN QUEEN)              ║
║  Rank: A (Shadow Form)                   ║
║  Skill: [ RADIANT PERSUASION ]           ║
║  - Can make any data look undeniable.    ║
║                                          ║
║  Shadow Slot: 2/2                        ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

The Shadow Cynthia looked at the real Cynthia, who was now fast asleep on her keyboard, her breathing deep and even. Then, the shadow looked at Juan. It bowed.

*"The Ferrari is a lie, boss,"* the shadow said, its voice like velvet. *"Shall we show them the engine instead?"*

Juan looked at the screen. "Show them everything, Cynthia. Use Rodel’s data forensics. Build the deck that Aldrich is too afraid to present."

The Shadow Cynthia turned to the keyboard. 

If Rodel was a machine gun, the Shadow Cynthia was a symphony. Her hands didn't just type; they danced. Slides were created, animated, and refined in seconds. Data visualizations bloomed on the screen like digital flowers, each one more convincing than the last. 

Juan sat in the neighboring chair, watching his two shadows work together. Rodel was feeding the raw, unedited truths from the Skyfall logs into Cynthia’s presentation engine, and Cynthia was turning them into a narrative of absolute competence.

```
║  STAMINA: 32%... 28%... 24%...           ║
```

The drain was immense. Maintaining two high-level shadows was pushing Juan to his limit. He felt his own vision start to blur, his heart racing in his chest.

But he didn't stop. 

By 3:00 AM, the presentation was finished. It was a masterpiece of corporate warfare—a deck that appeared to be exactly what Aldrich wanted on the surface, but contained a technical depth that would trap any executive who tried to question it.

Juan looked at the sleeping, real Cynthia. He reached out and gently closed her laptop.

"Go home, Cynthia," he whispered. 

The Shadow Cynthia merged back into the real woman's shadow. Juan stood up, his legs shaking, and began the long walk to the elevator.

He had his team. He had his weapon. 

Tomorrow, the pitch would begin. And Aldrich Santos would finally find out what happens when you try to ride a Ferrari built out of shadows.
