# Chapter 13 — Project Phoenix Day 1

*The first day of a project is the only time everyone is in a good mood. It’s the calm before the storm, the 'Hello World' before the 'Null Pointer Exception'. And for a System Worker, it’s the moment you realize your stamina bar is too small for your ambitions.*

---

The kickoff meeting for the implementation of Project Phoenix was held in the largest conference room on the 11th floor. It was no longer the obsidian-tabled aquarium of the 14th floor, but it was packed. 

For the first time in his life, Juan dela Cruz was sitting at the head of the table.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               PROJECT STATUS             ║
║                                          ║
║  Project: PHOENIX IMPLEMENTATION         ║
║  Phase: 1 (Infrastructure Setup)         ║
║  Team Morale: HIGH                       ║
║  Your Role: TECHNICAL LEAD               ║
║                                          ║
║  Active Shadows: 2/2                     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

"Okay, team," Juan said, his voice surprisingly steady. "We have forty-five days to build the loyalty engine. Phase 1 is the API backbone. We’re not doing Ferraris this time. We’re building a rocket."

He looked at the developers gathered around the table. They were mostly Rank-E and Rank-D associates, people who had been ignored for years. Now, they were looking at him with a mix of respect and desperation. He was the one who had beaten Aldrich. He was the one who had the "System."

"I’ve already mapped the first ten sprints," Juan continued, pointing to the screen where a perfectly organized Jira board was displayed—thanks to Rodel’s overnight work. "We’re going to work in parallel. Infrastructure, data migration, and frontend UI. No dependencies. No excuses."

The meeting was efficient. In twenty minutes, everyone had their tasks. As they filed out, Cynthia stayed behind. 

"You're a natural, Juan," she said, leaning against the doorframe. "You've given them hope. That’s a dangerous thing to do in this building."

"Hope is just another resource, ma'am," Juan said. "I'm just allocating it."

Cynthia smiled, but it didn't reach her eyes. She looked toward the glass wall of the conference room. Isabel Reyes was standing there, her charcoal suit a sharp contrast to the casual wear of the 11th floor. She was holding her tablet, her eyes fixed on Juan.

"She’s been here since 7 AM," Cynthia whispered. "She’s been auditing the server logs from last night. The ones from when you were 'finalizing' the deck."

```
║  ALERT: DETECTION METER: 22% [||       ] ║
║  Isabel Reyes has found 'Non-Human      ║
║  Processing Patterns' in the logs.       ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan felt a cold sweat prickle his neck. "I kept the documentation standard, ma'am."

"It doesn't matter. She doesn't care about the result. She cares about how you got there. She’s like a debugger for people, Juan. If she finds a bug in your behavior, she’ll delete you."

Juan stood up, gathering his notebook. "Then I’ll just have to make sure my behavior is perfectly optimized."

---

The rest of the day was a marathon of technical decisions and resource management. Juan was everywhere. He was debugging code for the juniors, reviewing database schemas for the seniors, and answering questions from the client.

Every time he used his shadows—Rodel for the data, Cynthia for the communication—he could feel the drain.

```
║  STAMINA: 15% [|        ]                ║
║  WARNING: CRITICAL EXHAUSTION IMMINENT   ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

At 6:00 PM, he retreated to his cubicle. The office was starting to thin out, but Isabel Reyes was still there. She had taken a seat in the back corner, near the printer. She wasn't doing anything. She was just... watching.

Juan closed his eyes, his head resting on his hand. 

"Rodel," he whispered. "We need to hide the traces. Delete any logs that show processing speeds higher than eighty words per minute."

*"Already on it, boss,"* Rodel’s voice was strained. *"But she’s using an external probe. She’s checking the physical power consumption of the server rack. We can't hide that."*

Juan opened his eyes. Power consumption. Of course. The shadows didn't just use his stamina; they used the office’s resources. And an audit specialist like Isabel would see the spike in electricity as clearly as a beacon.

He stood up and walked toward the breakroom. He needed more caffeine. 

As he passed the printer, Isabel Reyes stood up. 

"Mr. Dela Cruz," she said, her voice like silk over gravel.

Juan stopped. He didn't turn around immediately. He took a breath, letting his **[ CORPORATE INVISIBILITY ]** level 2 flare to its maximum.

"Yes, Ms. Reyes?"

"I’ve been looking at your performance metrics," she said, walking around to face him. She was shorter than him, but she seemed to fill the entire corridor. "You’ve increased the department’s output by three hundred percent in forty-eight hours. That’s statistically impossible for a team of your size and rank."

"We're motivated, ma'am. Project Phoenix is a big opportunity."

Isabel tapped her tablet. "Motivation doesn't explain how three thousand lines of clean, error-free code were committed to the repository between 2 AM and 4 AM last night. Code that uses architecture patterns that haven't been taught in any university for ten years."

She stepped closer, her eyes searching his face. 

"Patterns that Rodel Magsino used to use before he disappeared."

Juan didn't blink. "Rodel was a good analyst. We’re just building on his foundation."

Isabel’s smile was thin and dangerous. "I’m sure you are. But I’m curious, Mr. Dela Cruz. What happens when the foundation is removed? Or when the auditor decides to look under the floorboards?"

She turned and walked away, leaving Juan standing in the middle of the floor.

```
║  ALERT: DETECTION METER: 35% [|||      ] ║
║  Isabel Reyes has linked your patterns   ║
║  to 'The Fallen'.                        ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan walked into the breakroom and grabbed a cup of coffee. His hand was shaking. 

He had to move faster. He had to finish Phoenix before Isabel found the source of the shadows. Because if she found the source, she wouldn't just delete him. 

She would Raise him.
