# Chapter 6 — The 14th Floor

*In an office, the higher the floor, the thinner the air and the thicker the bullshit. But for a System Worker, the top floor is where the high-level loot is guarded by bosses who have forgotten what it’s like to use a printer.*

---

Juan dela Cruz stood in front of the elevator. 

He didn't press the button for the 1st floor. He didn't press the button for the 11th. He reached out and, with a hand that trembled only slightly, pressed '14'.

The elevator doors slid shut with a silence that cost more than his annual salary. 

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              ⚠ DANGER ZONE ⚠             ║
║                                          ║
║  Environment: THE EXECUTIVE SUITE        ║
║  Status: UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS             ║
║  Risk Level: CRITICAL                    ║
║                                          ║
║  Passive Skill Triggered:                ║
║  [ CORPORATE INVISIBILITY ] (Lvl 1)      ║
║  - Reduces detection by 30%              ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

The elevator climbed. Juan felt the weight of the building above him. He had worked at Pinnacle Systems for seven hundred and forty-six days, and he had never been higher than the 11th floor. To the people on the 14th, the 11th floor didn't exist; it was just a collection of numbers on a spreadsheet that they occasionally glanced at before lunch.

*Ding.*

The doors opened. 

The air on the 14th floor was different. It didn't smell like ozone and burnt coffee. It smelled of expensive wood polish, fresh lilies, and the kind of quiet that only comes from having thick carpets and zero accountability.

Juan stepped out. 

His **DEADLINE SENSE** was screaming. He had four hours left before the data audit, and the final piece of the Q3 report—the physical ledger of the offshore accounts—was somewhere in this maze of mahogany.

"Rodel," Juan whispered. "Scout the corridor. Find the archive room."

The shadow detached itself from Juan’s feet. It didn't crawl this time; it flowed along the baseboards like spilled ink, invisible against the dark wood. 

Juan walked slowly, his head down, looking at his notebook. He was playing the role of a 'Lost Delivery Guy' or 'Documentation Associate'—someone so unimportant that no one would bother to stop him.

*"Room 1408, boss,"* the shadow’s voice echoed in his mind. *"Behind the glass doors. There’s a guard. Not a security guard. A Secretary."*

Juan rounded the corner and saw her. 

She was sitting behind a desk made of a single slab of marble. She was Rank-A, according to the System. Her nameplate read: *MA. ELENA VIZCONDE — EXECUTIVE ASSISTANT*.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               BOSS ENCOUNTER             ║
║                                          ║
║  Name: ELENA (THE GATEKEEPER)            ║
║  Rank: A (Admin Specialist)              ║
║  Status: [ UNINTERRUPTIBLE ]             ║
║                                          ║
║  Special Attack: 'The Cold Stare'        ║
║  (Can freeze a Rank-F worker in place)   ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Elena didn't look up from her screen. She was typing with a rhythmic, lethal precision. 

Juan stopped ten feet away. He felt the 'Cold Stare' aura even before she looked at him. 

"I'm sorry," Juan said, his voice small and hesitant. "I'm from the 11th floor. IT Services. Aldrich Santos sent me to retrieve the Q2-Q3 Offshore Ledger for the audit."

Elena stopped typing. The silence that followed was heavy. She slowly raised her head. Her glasses caught the light of the chandelier, masking her eyes.

"Aldrich Santos," she said, her voice like a chilled blade. "Did Aldrich provide a signed Requisition Form 12-B?"

"He... he said it was urgent. He didn't have time to sign the form," Juan lied. Aldrich hadn't sent him at all. Aldrich was currently at a three-hour lunch, confident that Juan was already failing.

Elena leaned back. "No form, no ledger. This is the Executive Floor, Mr...?"

"Dela Cruz."

"Mr. Dela Cruz. We don't do 'urgent' here. We do 'correct'. Come back when you have the signature."

She went back to her typing. The encounter was over. 

```
║  DETECTION METER: 45% [||||      ]       ║
```

Juan felt the pressure. If he stayed, the meter would rise. If he left, the report would fail. 

*"Boss,"* Rodel’s shadow whispered from beneath the marble desk. *"She has the key in her top drawer. But she never leaves the desk. She’s a machine."*

Juan looked at Elena. She wasn't a machine. She was a Rank-A worker. And every worker has a breaking point.

"Elena," Juan said, his voice slightly louder. "I know the protocol. But if this ledger isn't in the report by tomorrow, the board is going to ask why the Offshore accounts weren't audited. And they’re going to ask who was the last person to hold the key."

Elena’s typing faltered for a fraction of a second.

"Is that a threat, Mr. Dela Cruz?"

"No," Juan said, stepping closer, his shadow stretching out toward the desk. "It’s a documentation. I’m just recording the dependency. If the ledger is missing, the failure is tracked back to Room 1408. My report already has a placeholder for 'Access Denied by Executive Admin'."

Elena looked at him again. This time, she didn't use the 'Cold Stare'. She looked at him with the curiosity one shows a cockroach that has suddenly started speaking Latin.

"Aldrich Santos is a snake," she said quietly. "He’s setting you up."

"I know," Juan said. "That’s why I need the ledger."

Elena stared at him for a long, agonizing minute. Then, without a word, she opened the top drawer, pulled out a brass key, and slid it across the marble.

"You have ten minutes. If that ledger isn't back by then, I’m calling Security and telling them you stole it."

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               BOSS DEFEATED              ║
║                                          ║
║  Victory!                                ║
║  Loot Dropped: [ ARCHIVE KEY ]           ║
║  Exp Gained: 500                         ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan grabbed the key. "Thank you, Elena."

He turned and practically ran toward Room 1408. 

He didn't see the small smile that touched Elena’s lips as she went back to her typing. Or the way the System notification changed.

```
║  Status: ELENA (THE GATEKEEPER)          ║
║  New Affinity: [ RECOGNITION ]           ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan entered the archive. It was a tomb of paper. He found the ledger in less than a minute. It was heavy, bound in dark leather, and smelled of old secrets.

As he reached for it, a new window appeared.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              ⚠ HIDDEN ITEM ⚠             ║
║                                          ║
║  [ THE SKYFALL LOGS ]                    ║
║  Hidden behind the Offshore Ledger.      ║
║  Description: The original, unedited     ║
║  report of why the migration failed.     ║
║                                          ║
║  Do you take it?                         ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan froze. These were the logs that would prove who was really responsible for the crash that nearly broke the company. The logs that Aldrich Santos and the other S-Ranks had spent weeks trying to hide.

His **DEADLINE SENSE** was pulsing. 

*Time left: 28:15:00.*

If he took the logs, he was no longer just a worker. He was a threat.

Juan reached out. He took the ledger. 

And then, he took the logs.
