# Chapter 4 — The First Daily Mission

*In the corporate world, the reward for doing a good job is more work. In the System, the reward for doing an impossible job is a set of stats that make the work feel like a game.*

---

The sun was rising over BGC, casting long, sharp shadows across the glass towers of the city. For most of the employees at Pinnacle Systems, the morning light was a signal to crawl into their workstations and begin the slow, steady grind.

For Juan dela Cruz, it was the start of his first official day as a System Worker.

He stood in front of the office bathroom mirror at 8:14 AM. He had splashed cold water on his face, but it didn't do much for the dark circles under his eyes. He had been in the building for twenty-four hours.

A small, blue window was hovering in the corner of his vision.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                 DAILY MISSION                ║
║                                              ║
║  [ THE SURVIVALIST'S MORNING ]               ║
║  1. Log in before 8:30 AM (08:14) [✓]        ║
║  2. Consume one (1) unit of caffeine [ ]     ║
║  3. Complete morning standup without         ║
║     showing fatigue. [ ]                     ║
║                                              ║
║  REWARD: +1 STAMINA, +50 EXP                 ║
║  PENALTY: SURPRISE PERFORMANCE REVIEW        ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

"Surprise performance review," Juan muttered, drying his hands with a rough paper towel. "The System really knows how to threaten a Filipino employee."

He walked to the breakroom. The coffee dispenser was hissing, a sound that usually meant it was about to die or it was brewing something that tasted like burnt rubber. He filled a paper cup, watched the brown liquid swirl, and took a sip.

```
║  2. Consume one (1) unit of caffeine [✓]     ║
```

The caffeine hit his system like a low-voltage shock. He felt his STAMINA bar flicker, but it didn't rise. He was running on fumes, and the System knew it.

---

The 9:00 AM meeting was held in Conference Room 11-A.

The atmosphere was electric, but not in a good way. The success of the "Project Skyfall" migration had saved the company from immediate disaster, but it had also revealed a gaping hole in the department’s resources.

Rodel Magsino was gone.

His empty chair in the meeting room was a physical weight. Dennis Soriano sat at the head of the table, looking like he hadn't slept in a week—which was true. Beside him was Aldrich Santos, the Rank-S Senior Manager who had been "networking" while the servers were melting.

Aldrich looked perfect. His barong was crisp, his hair was gelled to perfection, and he smelled of expensive cologne.

"First of all," Aldrich said, his voice smooth and authoritative, "I want to congratulate everyone on the successful migration. The client is... satisfied. Mostly."

He paused, letting the word 'mostly' hang in the air like a threat.

"However," Aldrich continued, "the Q3 report is due tomorrow. And because of the 'incident' last night, we are missing the lead analyst who was supposed to compile the data."

He looked around the room, his eyes skipping over the Rank-E and Rank-F employees until they settled on Dennis.

"Dennis, who do we have?"

Dennis looked like he wanted to crawl under the table. "Well, Rodel was the only one who really understood the legacy data mapping. Without him, it'll take at least a week to run the aggregations."

"We don't have a week," Aldrich said flatly. "The board needs the numbers by tomorrow at 5 PM. If we miss this, the Skyfall success won't matter. We'll be seen as a company that can fix a fire but can't keep the lights on."

The room went cold.

"I can do it."

The voice was quiet. It didn't belong to a manager. It didn't belong to a senior.

Everyone turned to look at the corner of the room.

Juan dela Cruz was sitting there, his notebook open. He wasn't looking at Aldrich; he was looking at a window that had just appeared in front of him.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              ⚠ QUEST TRIGGERED ⚠             ║
║                                              ║
║  [ DUNGEON: THE Q3 REPORT ]                  ║
║  Rank: E (Difficulty: High for Rank-F)       ║
║                                              ║
║  Objective: Compile and submit the Q3        ║
║  Report with 98% accuracy.                   ║
║  TIME LIMIT: 32:00:00                        ║
║                                              ║
║  REWARD:                                     ║
║  - Rank Up to E                              ║
║  - New Shadow Slot                           ║
║  - Skill: [ CORPORATE INVISIBILITY ]         ║
║                                              ║
║  PENALTY: IMMEDIATE TERMINATION              ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Aldrich Santos squinted at Juan. "You... you're the documentation associate, right? Juan... something?"

"Juan dela Cruz, sir. Rank F," Juan said, standing up.

"And you think you can do the work of a Senior Analyst? In thirty-two hours?" Aldrich’s smile was thin, the kind of smile a cat gives a mouse it’s about to eat.

"I have the notes from the migration," Juan said. "I know where the data is. And I've already prepared a preliminary template."

Dennis looked at Juan, his eyes wide. "Juan, are you sure? This isn't just data entry. This is high-level analysis."

"I'm sure, sir."

Aldrich leaned back, tapping his pen against the table. "Fine. If you’re so confident, Juan dela Cruz, the report is yours. But let's be clear: if you fail, you aren't just failing yourself. You're failing the entire division. And I don't keep failures on my payroll."

"Understood, sir."

```
║  3. Complete morning standup without         ║
║     showing fatigue. [✓]                     ║
║                                              ║
║  DAILY MISSION CLEAR!                        ║
║  Rewards: +1 STAMINA, +50 EXP                ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

As the meeting broke up, the other employees hurried out, some giving Juan looks of pity, others looks of annoyance. They saw a man who had just committed professional suicide.

Juan walked back to his desk. Station 11-47.

He sat down. He didn't open Excel. He didn't look at the data.

Instead, he closed his eyes and felt the presence in his shadow.

*Rodel,* he thought. *Are you ready?*

The shadow beneath his feet flickered, a dark, jagged shape that only he could see.

*"Ready, boss,"* the whisper echoed in his mind.

Juan opened his laptop.

"Then let's go into the dungeon."
