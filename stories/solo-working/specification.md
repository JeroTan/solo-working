# Story Specification — Solo Working

---

## Logline

A perpetually-overlooked Rank-F employee awakens a mysterious Work System that lets him raise burned-out coworkers as shadow workers — and begins his ruthless, spreadsheet-assisted ascent to become the most feared professional in the company.

---

## Premise

**Protagonist:** Juan dela Cruz — a 27-year-old rank-F associate at a mid-sized IT firm in BGC, Manila. He has been passed over for every promotion, assigned to every task no one else wants, and earns the minimum wage the company can legally justify. He is, by every measurable metric, the worst employee at Pinnacle Systems Inc.

**Goal:** Survive. Then succeed. Then become untouchable.

**Conflict:** After being the sole survivor of a catastrophic "office dungeon" — a company-wide system migration that went spectacularly wrong and mentally broke every other employee assigned to it — Juan finds himself changed. The Work System awakens inside him. What follows is not a fairy tale. It is a grind.

**Stakes:** [Core Requirement] If Juan fails to complete System-assigned quests, he suffers real, debilitating penalties. Fail a deadline: -20 FOCUS for 48 hours. Miss a DAILY MISSION: spawns a "Shadow Audit" — a surprise task that appears at the worst possible moment. Die (i.e., resign or be terminated) before reaching Rank-S: the System resets. He goes back to zero. He will not let that happen.

---

## One-Page Summary

**Setting:** Pinnacle Systems Inc., a mid-tier IT services company in BGC, Taguig. 11th floor. The air conditioning is always slightly too cold. The printer is always slightly broken.

**Core Story:** Juan dela Cruz has always been invisible. Assigned the grunt work — data encoding, meeting minutes, cable management. He is the last person anyone would bet on. Then the incident happens.

During a high-stakes, all-hands system migration — a project the company dubbed "Project Skyfall" (internally, employees called it something unprintable) — something goes wrong. The servers cascade. The data corrupts. One by one, every team member breaks: panic attacks, rage-quits, or simply stares at their screen with empty eyes. Juan alone remains. He does not know why. He does not feel fear. He only feels the cursor blinking.

And then a window appears. Only he can see it.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║           SYSTEM INITIALIZATION          ║
║                                          ║
║  A new Worker has been detected.         ║
║  Rank: F                                 ║
║  Unique Ability: RAISE                   ║
║                                          ║
║  The System welcomes you.                ║
║  Complete your tasks.                    ║
║  Or don't. It won't matter soon anyway.  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

From that point forward, Juan navigates office life as a man with a hidden power. He accepts quests. He completes daily missions. He raises the fallen — coworkers shattered by the corporate grind — transforming them into Shadow Workers under his command, loyal beyond reason, competent beyond human limits.

Each arc of the story is a "dungeon" — a high-stakes project or corporate crisis that Juan and his growing shadow workforce must conquer. With each dungeon cleared, his rank rises. With each rank, new threats emerge. Higher-ranked employees who see him as a threat. Executives who want to control the System. And somewhere at the top of the corporate ladder — a final boss whose identity Juan has not yet uncovered.

**Target Audience:** Adults 18–35, especially Filipinos with corporate work experience. Fans of Solo Leveling, office comedy, and Filipino-flavored dark humor. Readers who have ever muttered _"Putangina nito"_ at a spreadsheet.

**Success Criteria:**

- Readers feel the absurdity AND the genuine tension simultaneously
- Each dungeon arc has a satisfying payoff
- **"Rise."** and **"Raise!"** become the two signature lines readers wait for — one cold, one chaotic

## Full Specification

### Act Structure (Web Serial / Arc-Based)

#### Arc 0 — Rank F: The Invisible Employee _(Prologue + Chapters 1–3)_

- Establish Juan's world: the office, the hierarchy, his invisibility, his quietly competent desperation
- The Project Skyfall incident — the dungeon that breaks everyone except him
- System initialization. First quest. First failure. First lesson.
- Tone goal: _Oppressive normalcy followed by a crack of something impossible._

#### Arc 1 — The First Dungeon: The Q3 Report _(Chapters 4–10)_

- Juan's first System-assigned dungeon: a quarterly report that is, somehow, due tomorrow
- He has no data. He has no help. He has no time.
- He completes it. Imperfectly. But he completes it.
- REWARD: First Shadow Worker slot unlocked. He raises his first worker — a data analyst named Rodel who had a breakdown and hasn't spoken since.
- First use of **"Rise."** — Rodel appears from the shadows of the server room. Rodel delivers. The pivot table is corrected.
- Note: "Raise!" was used prior to this moment, in the dark, when no one was watching. That scene is Arc 1's turning point.
- Tone goal: _The first taste of power. It tastes like instant coffee and moral ambiguity._

#### Arc 2 — The Client Pitch: Dungeon Boss Appears _(Chapters 11–20)_

- A major client pitch is assigned to Juan's team. The client is unreasonable. The senior assigned to lead it is incompetent and politically protected.
- Juan must navigate corporate politics _while_ secretly engineering the pitch's success from below.
- He raises a second Shadow Worker: Cynthia, a former presentation queen who burned out after being sidelined.
- The dungeon boss: Senior Manager Aldrich Santos — charismatic, well-connected, threatened by Juan's quiet competence.
- Resolution: Juan wins the pitch. Santos takes credit. Juan's rank upgrades to E. He expected this. He planned for it.
- Tone goal: _Righteousness weaponized as patience._

#### Arc 3 — The Audit: Enter the System Hunter _(Chapters 21–30)_

- An external audit is triggered — someone on the executive floor is investigating the anomaly: one Rank-F employee who has never missed a deadline, never failed to deliver, and somehow commands results that should be statistically impossible.
- A corporate "hunter" (internal investigator) named Isabel Reyes is assigned to find out what Juan is.
- Isabel is smart. Isabel is thorough. Isabel is also — the System quietly notes — capable of being Raised.
- Juan must complete the audit, survive Isabel's investigation, and manage three active Shadow Workers simultaneously.
- Tone goal: _The protagonist is now the dungeon._

- The identity of the "Final Boss Executive" is seeded from Arc 1 onward
- The question of what happens when a Shadow Worker wants to become free — [Needs Clarification]

### Characters

#### Juan dela Cruz — Protagonist

- Age: 27
- Title: Associate, IT Services Division (Rank F → rising)
- Appearance: Unremarkable by design. Medium height. The kind of face people forget at a meeting. [Core Requirement] He does not change his appearance as he grows stronger. Power is internal.
- Personality: Quiet. Methodical. Carries a small notebook. Has never raised his voice in the office. Has thought about it exactly 1,247 times.
- Backstory: [Needs Clarification] — needs more detail beyond "struggling employee." What drove him to accept the System so calmly?
- Signature: Submits everything before 4:59 PM.

#### Rodel Magsino — First Shadow Worker

- Specialty: Data analysis, Excel, the dark arts of VLOOKUP
- Background: Former top analyst. Burned out after working 72-hour weeks on a project that got cancelled. Now sits at his desk and stares.
- Post-Raise: Precise, loyal, speaks only in data. _"The variance is 3.4%. You should know this."_
- Parody note: He is the most terrifying person in any meeting because he is _always right._

#### Cynthia Ocampo — Second Shadow Worker

- Specialty: Presentations, client communication, the ability to make terrible ideas look beautiful
- Background: Was once the best presenter in the company. Was sidelined for a younger, male colleague. Never recovered professionally.
- Post-Raise: Elegant, focused, merciless in critique. _"Slide 7 is weak. I'll fix it. Do not look at Slide 7 again."_

#### Aldrich Santos — Arc 2 Antagonist

- Title: Senior Manager, Business Development
- The high-ranked obstacle. Politically bulletproof. Genuinely charismatic. Not evil — just in the way.
- His flaw: He relies on his connections, not his competence. A real dungeon tests competence.

#### Isabel Reyes — Arc 3 Antagonist / Potential Ally

- Title: Internal Audit Specialist
- The investigator. She does not use the System but she doesn't need to. She uses logic.
- [Needs Clarification] — Is she ultimately raised? Or does she become the first person who knows Juan's secret and stays human?

#### The Final Boss — [Needs Clarification]

- Identity TBD. Seeded in Arc 1 as "the executive who approved Project Skyfall."
- [Needs Clarification] — What do they want? Do they know about the System? Did they _cause_ the System?

### System Mechanics Reference

```
STATS:
  FOCUS          ← Concentration, accuracy, resistance to distraction
  STAMINA        ← Physical and mental endurance (depletes with overtime)
  NETWORKING     ← Passive skill: knowing who to know
  PRESENTATION   ← Verbal and visual communication effectiveness
  DEADLINE SENSE ← Passive: intuition for time pressure and prioritization
  CORPORATE SURVIVAL ← Composite stat: politics, adaptability, threat detection

QUEST TYPES:
  [MAIN QUEST]    ← Arc-defining objectives (complete the dungeon)
  [SIDE QUEST]    ← Optional tasks with minor rewards
  [DAILY MISSION] ← Recurring obligations (morning standup, inbox zero, etc.)
  [HIDDEN QUEST]  ← Triggered by specific conditions; high risk, high reward

PENALTIES:
  MISSED DEADLINE   → -20 FOCUS, 48-hour debuff
  FAILED DAILY      → Shadow Audit spawned (surprise urgent task)
  PUBLIC FAILURE    → -15 CORPORATE SURVIVAL (permanent unless recovered)

RAISE REQUIREMENTS:
  Target must be: (a) a competent worker, (b) broken by corporate conditions
  Target must have been witnessed by Juan at moment of breaking
  Consent is... ambiguous. The System does not ask.
```
