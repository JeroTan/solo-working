# Cover Art Prompt — Solo Working

## Prompt

This is cover art for "Solo Working" — a Filipino office parody novel directly inspired by the manhwa "Solo Leveling." Where Solo Leveling follows a hunter awakening in a world of monsters and dungeons, Solo Working follows a Rank-F corporate employee awakening a Work System inside a Manila office building. The visual language, tone, and dramatic weight must mirror Solo Leveling's iconic cover art aesthetic — but every element is replaced with its corporate equivalent: dungeons become project deadlines, shadow soldiers become shadow workers, and the dark gate is the elevator to the 11th floor.

Digital illustration, novel cover art style. A young Filipino man, late 20s, lean and sharp-eyed, stands alone at the center of a dimly lit corporate office floor at night. He wears a slightly rumpled white button-down shirt, dark slacks, and an employee lanyard. His posture is utterly still — not triumphant, not tense. Just calm. The kind of calm that comes after surviving something no one else did. His eyes carry a cold, quiet intensity staring directly forward.

Behind him, the open-plan office stretches into shadow: rows of dark cubicles, glowing monitors, a flickering fluorescent light overhead. The ceiling is low. The atmosphere is oppressive. Through the far windows, the nighttime skyline of BGC, Taguig — glass towers, orange city glow — bleeds in from outside.

Emerging from the deep shadows between the cubicles, barely visible, are silhouetted human figures — standing at attention, facing him. They are rendered in muted dark blue and deep gray, like echoes of workers who no longer need rest. One holds a laptop. One holds a stack of folders. They are still. They are ready.

Above the man's head floats a barely-visible translucent blue HUD window — a system status notification panel rendered in clean monospace font, glowing faintly like a hologram. The text inside reads: **RANK: F**

The lighting is harsh fluorescent from above — white and merciless — casting sharp shadows downward on his face. The floor reflects a faint green tint from the monitor glow. The overall color palette is cold: deep navy, charcoal gray, white fluorescent, with one streak of amber from the city outside.

Composition: vertical portrait format (2:3 ratio, suitable for a book cover). The man is positioned in the lower-center foreground, looking up slightly. The title space is left clear at the top of the image.

Style: cinematic manhwa illustration, high contrast, sharp linework, inspired by Solo Leveling's visual language but set entirely in a modern corporate office environment. Photorealistic shading with stylized outlines. Dramatic. Dark. Filipino soul.

**No text. No watermarks. No logos.**

## Title Placement Note

After generating, the title **SOLO WORKING** should be placed at the top in bold angular font (like a manhwa title treatment), and a subtitle or author name can go at the bottom. Leave visual breathing room at top and bottom of the composition.

## Optional Variation Prompts

Behind him, only darkness and the faint outline of one seated shadow worker at a computer. Cold. Minimal. Haunting.
