## Character Overview

**Name:** Isabel Reyes
**Role:** Arc 3 Antagonist / Potential Ally — Internal Audit Specialist
**Novel:** Solo Working (Filipino office parody of Solo Leveling)

---

## Prompt

Character illustration for "Solo Working," a Filipino office parody manhwa-style novel. The character is Isabel Reyes — Internal Audit Specialist assigned to investigate the anomaly that is Juan dela Cruz. She does not have the System. She does not need it. She uses logic, and it is enough.

**Physical appearance:** Filipino woman, late 20s to early 30s. Medium height. Her appearance is deliberately chosen for invisibility — like Juan, she understands the strategic value of being underestimated. But unlike Juan, her composure reads as active intelligence rather than passive survival. Sharp eyes. The kind that index everything they land on. She does not miss things. She has never missed a thing.

**Clothing:** A structured blazer — deep burgundy, olive, or dark teal — over a white blouse. Pressed trousers. Practical, professional heels. A lanyard with her audit credentials. She carries a document folder — not a tablet, not a laptop. Paper. She trusts documents she can hold. She does not trust systems she cannot audit.

**Posture:** Precise and controlled. She stands or sits with the economy of motion of a person who has learned to make every action deliberate. She is never restless. When she is still, she is *thinking.*

**Expression:** Neutral to the outside observer. Analytically alive beneath it. Her eyes are the tell — they are always two questions ahead of whatever is being said. She is interviewing you right now. She started the moment you walked in.

**Props:** A pen in hand — not a gesture, ready to write. A document folder under one arm. Optional: glasses, either worn or held in the other hand, which she uses as a thinking prop.

**Lighting:** Split lighting — one side harsh fluorescent (the office world she navigates), the other in softer shadow (the unknown she is approaching). She exists at the boundary between known and unknown, which is exactly where auditors live.

**Background:** A hint of an audit workspace — files, a neat desk, a whiteboard with a diagram (abstract, unreadable). Or a corridor — neutral, in-between. She is always between places, between conversations, between certainties.

**Mood:** The most dangerous person in the building who is not Juan. She is not his enemy. She is the first person who might understand him — and that makes her the most threatening presence he has faced. Whether she becomes his greatest threat or his first real ally is yet to be determined.

**Style:** Cinematic manhwa illustration. High contrast lines. Filipino facial features. She should feel like the kind of person who walks into a scene and immediately changes the power dynamic — not through force, but through *comprehension.*

**No text. No watermarks. No logos.**

## Variant Prompts

**Variant A — The Report:**
Isabel at a desk, document folder open in front of her. She is reading. Her pen is poised above a page with handwritten notes — already dense with observations. One note is circled. It refers to Juan dela Cruz. Her expression has not changed. But the circle is drawn twice.

**Variant B — First Encounter:**
Isabel and Juan, facing each other in a corridor. Neither is threatening the other. Neither is backing down. The fluorescent light is between them, one tube flickering. They are both very still. The shot is symmetrical. The question of who is the hunter and who is the dungeon is deliberately unanswered.

Isabel walking away from frame, seen from behind. Beside her, barely visible — a translucent blue system notification panel (Juan's HUD, visible only to him, to the viewer). The text on the panel reads: `TARGET DETECTED. STATUS: CAPABLE. CLASSIFICATION: [PENDING]`
