## Prompt

Character illustration for "Solo Working," a Filipino office parody manhwa-style novel. The character is Dennis Soriano — Manager of IT Services, Rank C. He is the quintessential middle manager: competent but tired, efficient but uninspired, and possessed of a legendary talent for delegation.

**Physical appearance:** Filipino male, early 40s. Average height. He has a slightly soft build, the "manager's belly" of a man who eats breakfast at his desk. His hair is black and thinning at the temples. His eyes have permanent bags under them, suggesting years of late-night deployments and early-morning status calls.

**Clothing:** A light blue short-sleeved collared shirt — slightly rumpled at the waist where it's tucked into his dark slacks. He wears a company ID lanyard (slightly crooked). His appearance is "standard corporate" — not as polished as the executives, but more professional than the junior devs.

**Posture:** The "Manager's Slump." He stands with his shoulders slightly forward, perhaps checking his watch with a look of mild annoyance. He looks like he’s constantly calculating how many more minutes until he can reasonably leave for the day.

**Expression:** Weary resignation. He has a neutral, almost bored face that he uses to deflect extra work. However, when things get critical, his eyes sharpen, revealing the hidden "True Rank: B" competence he usually keeps hidden to avoid being assigned more projects.

**Lighting:** Harsh fluorescent office lighting. Cold, overhead light that emphasizes the tired lines of his face and the slightly rumpled texture of his shirt. Shadows are functional and unromantic.

**Background:** A cluttered office environment. Cubicle walls, a stack of printer paper, or a "World's Okayest Boss" mug on a desk in the background.

**Mood:** The man who keeps the gears turning while pretending he’s just watching them spin. He is the anchor for Juan's team — the one who provides the cover of "standard procedure" so Juan can work his magic.

**Style:** Cinematic manhwa illustration. High contrast. Filipino facial features. He should look like a real person you’d meet in a Makati or BGC office — recognizable, relatable, and deeply tired.
