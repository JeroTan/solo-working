## Prompt

Character illustration for "Solo Working," a Filipino office parody manhwa-style novel. The character is Aldrich Santos — Senior Manager of Business Development, Rank A, and the primary antagonist of Arc 2. He is not a villain. He is something more frustrating: a competent-looking man coasting entirely on connections.

**Physical appearance:** Filipino male, late 30s to early 40s. Tall, well-built — not from the gym, from genetics. Handsome in the way that photographs well at company events. His smile reaches his eyes automatically, trained over a career of lunches and dinners with clients. His hair is styled with effort that looks effortless. He looks like a LinkedIn profile that actually worked.

**Clothing:** A well-fitted suit — not too expensive, not too modest. Exactly calibrated to communicate _senior manager_ without threatening anyone higher up. A pocket square. Cufflinks — tasteful, not flashy. His shoes are polished. Everything about him says: _I belong in this room._ Nothing about him says: _I earned it._

**Posture:** Relaxed confidence. He leans slightly, one hand in his pocket, the other holding — depending on the scene — a coffee cup, a phone, or nothing at all. He takes up space without appearing to. He has been doing this for years.

**Expression:** Warm. The kind of warm that makes you feel seen for exactly as long as he needs you to feel seen. His smile is calibrated. His eye contact is practiced. He is not insincere — he genuinely likes people. He just likes himself more.

**Lighting:** Warm, soft lighting — conference room ambient, the kind of light that makes everyone look good in photo ops. Unlike Juan and the Shadow Workers, who are lit by harsh fluorescent or cold screen glow, Santos lives in the warmth of favorable optics.

**Background:** A blurred conference room setting, or the open-plan floor of a well-lit office. Optional: a projected slide in the background with his name somewhere on it.

**Mood:** The antagonist who will never understand why he lost. He is not the darkness. He is the obstacle between the protagonist and the light — and he is smiling the entire time.

**Style:** Cinematic manhwa illustration. High contrast. Filipino facial features. He should look like a Rank-A employee — polished, substantial, credible. The danger is not in his face. It is in the system that rewards it.

**No text. No watermarks. No logos.**
