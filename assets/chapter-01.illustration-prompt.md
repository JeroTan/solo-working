## Illustration #1 — Morning Ritual: The White Shirt

**Location in Chapter:** After epigraph, before "He rose without ceremony"

**Scene Description:**
Juan's studio apartment, early morning. Cold light from a single window. He is standing at an ironing board in his tiny kitchen area, ironing the same white button-down shirt he wears every Monday. His notebook sits on the small counter beside him, spine visible, already filled with notes. A simple breakfast setup visible in the background: rice from last night, a fried egg on a plate, no condiments.

**Visual Elements:**

- Single white button-down shirt on the ironing board, crisp and clean
- Steam rising from the iron (warm detail in cold light)
- His hands: precise, controlled, methodical
- The notebook: small, black, worn, organized
- Minimal furniture. Bare walls. The room feels like a tactical headquarters, not a home
- Lighting: cold blue-gray dawn light through a small window, creating harsh shadows

**Mood:**
Preparation. Not resignation. The visual language of someone getting ready for war, but with mundane tools. There is no drama — only discipline.

**Style Reference:**
Solo Leveling character preparation scenes. Realistic shading with stylized linework. Focus on the hands and the shirt as metaphors for control.

**Optional Composition:**
Wide shot showing the full tiny apartment, emphasizing the scale of his isolation. Or close-up on his face in profile, expression blank, the iron in foreground reflected in a mirror.

## Illustration #2 — The 11th Floor: Office Dungeon

**Location in Chapter:** At "The 11th floor was already lit when Juan stepped off the elevator"

**Scene Description:**
The open-plan office floor. Rows of cubicles stretching into shadow. Harsh fluorescent overhead lights — one tube flickering slightly. In the foreground: Juan's workstation. He is sitting down, logging in, notebook already beside his keyboard. The office is mostly empty at this early hour — only a few other early arrivals, silhouetted, at their desks. A dying potted plant sits near the elevators. The motivational poster is visible: "_EXCELLENCE IS A HABIT — Management_" on a faded wall.

**Visual Elements:**

- Fluorescent tube lights — harsh, white, merciless. One should flicker
- Rows of dark monitors, most powered down
- Juan at his workstation, small against the space, expression focused
- The dying plant: visual metaphor for corporate neglect
- The motivational poster, visible but ignored
- BGC skyline distantly visible through far windows, city glow but very distant
- Cool color palette: gray, dark blue, harsh white light, sickly green monitor glow

**Mood:**
Oppressive. Not hostile, exactly. Just _demanding._ The space has weight. It is indifferent to the people in it.

**Style Reference:**
Cinematic establishing shot. Wide perspective, high contrast. The office as a dungeon — not dramatic, just structurally true.

**Composition:**
Shot from above or from the side showing depth and scale. Juan in the mid-foreground, desk, then stretching rows of cubicles into shadow.

## Illustration #3 — Manager Arrival: The Hierarchy

**Location in Chapter:** At "At 9:15 AM, his manager, Dennis Soriano, arrived"

**Scene Description:**
Two-shot: Dennis Soriano arriving at his desk (one row ahead of Juan's), setting down his bag without awareness. Juan looks up from his screen. They are in the middle of the office floor, routine but tense. Dennis is rumpled, casual, disconnected. Juan is sitting upright, already focused. The contrast is immediate.

**Visual Elements:**

- Dennis: collared shirt (blue), casual posture, bag slung without care, holding coffee
- Juan: white shirt, sitting upright, hands on keyboard, eyes looking up, expression unreadable
- The desks: organized vs. slightly cluttered visual language
- Fluorescent light from above
- Other workers in background, blurred, faceless

**Mood:**
The visual language of power dynamics without words. Juan's readiness vs. Dennis's ease. The protagonist's contained intensity vs. the manager's obliviousness.

**Style Reference:**
Manhwa character interaction scene. Close enough to show expression, compositionally showing the hierarchy of attention (Dennis unaware, Juan aware).

**Composition:**
Two-character shot, slightly angled to emphasize the distance and difference between their desk layouts and postures. Could be side-by-side or slightly overlapped.

## Illustration #4 — Conference Room: The Catalyst

**Location in Chapter:** At "The man at the front was Raymond Lim, Rank-A Vice President of Operations"

**Scene Description:**
Inside Conference Room 11-A. Raymond Lim stands at the front near the projector, comfortable and confidently explaining Project Skyfall. The assembled team is seated around the conference table in unconscious hierarchy: Rank A/B closest to the screen, Rank C/D in middle, Rank E/F at the sides and back. Juan is seated nearest the door, notebook already in hand. The projector is lit, showing the presentation slide: "_PROJECT SKYFALL: ENTERPRISE SYSTEM MIGRATION — PHASE 1 KICKOFF_". The atmosphere is tense — people are processing the implications.

**Visual Elements:**

- Raymond Lim at the front, professional, pleasant, unaware of the crisis
- Silhouetted faces of the assembled team around the table
- Juan in the foreground, edge of frame, notebook open, pen in hand — already calculating
- The projection slide visible on the wall behind Raymond
- Cold fluorescent light from above, warm projector light from the front
- The coffee cups (one mentioned as stale in the text) should be visible on the table
- Worn whiteboard with old notes
- Stale atmosphere made visual

**Mood:**
The moment before catastrophe. No one knows yet. But Juan is already seeing it. The pivot point of the chapter.

**Style Reference:**
Group scene with protagonist in foreground. High dramatic tension rendered through posture and lighting. Solo Leveling style: the moment of selection/realization.

**Composition:**
Shot from Juan's POV or slightly from behind him, looking toward Raymond Lim and the team. The hierarchy of seating visible. The power structure crystallized in one image.

## Illustration #5 — The Calculation: Notebook Close-Up

**Location in Chapter:** At "He turned to a fresh page. He began to plan."

**Scene Description:**

- The pen: black, poised, ready
- His hand: steady, precise, methodical
- His face: half in shadow, half in fluorescent light, expression unreadable
- Eyes: focused, already processing the solution pattern
- Background: conference room blurred, other people blurred, but the notebook is sharp

**Mood:**
Quiet competence. Foreshadowing. This is before the System. But he is already operating like a hunter. Before he understands the war, he has already begun planning it.

**Style Reference:**
Close-up character moment. Manhwa detail shot. Focus on the hands and the notebook as indicators of his nature. The calm before everything changes.

**Composition:**
Top-down or angle shot emphasizing the notebook and hand. His face visible in edge frame, focused on the work, not on us watching him.

## Technical Notes for Illustration Generation

**Color Palette (All Illustrations):**

- Primary: Cold fluorescent white, dark gray, charcoal
- Secondary: Deep blue, navy, dark teal
- Accent: Sickly green (monitor glow), warm amber (city lights)
- Warm light only in warm scenes (none in this chapter — everything is cold/clinical)

**Lighting Consistency:**

- Fluorescent overhead → harsh white, strong shadows downward
- Monitor glow → cold blue-green
- Dawn light → cold blue-gray
- City glow in distance → warm amber, very distant

**Character Design References:**

- Juan dela Cruz: [See `juan-dela-cruz.prompt.md` — Everyday Moment variant]
- Dennis Soriano: Rumpled, casual, checked shirt (blue), unremarkable
- Raymond Lim: Confident, well-dressed, pleasant, polished appearance

**Artistic Style:**

- Cinematic manhwa illustration
- High contrast lines with photorealistic shading
- Filipino facial features
- Solo Leveling visual language applied to corporate setting
- No exaggeration — dignity in the mundane
