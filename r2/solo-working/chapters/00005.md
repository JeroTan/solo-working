# Chapter 5 — Shadow Analysis

*The hardest part of a dungeon isn't the final boss. It’s the hidden traps, the poison status effects, and the 'vlookup' errors that only appear when you're three minutes away from a deadline.*

---

Juan dela Cruz stared at his monitor. 

On the surface, it was just a standard workstation: a black Dell monitor, a slightly greasy keyboard, and a mouse that occasionally decided to move in the opposite direction. But through the lens of the System, the desk was transformed.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               DUNGEON STATUS             ║
║                                          ║
║  Environment: THE DATA VOID              ║
║  Hazard Level: MODERATE (Information     ║
║  Overload)                               ║
║  Active Buffs: [ DEADLINE SENSE ]        ║
║                                          ║
║  Primary Objective: RECONSTRUCT Q3       ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

The "Data Void" was a perfect description. To compile the Q3 report, Juan had to pull data from four different legacy systems that didn't talk to each other, a shared spreadsheet that had been corrupted by thirty different users, and a handwritten ledger that was currently sitting in a locked cabinet in the 14th-floor archive.

"Rodel," Juan whispered.

The shadow beneath his feet didn't just flicker; it expanded. It crawled up the back of his chair and merged with his own shadow on the monitor. 

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║             SHADOW SKILL ACTIVE          ║
║                                          ║
║       [ MULTI-THREADED ANALYSIS ]        ║
║                                          ║
║  Effect: Rodel can process up to 3       ║
║  data streams simultaneously.            ║
║  Cost: 2 STAMINA / MINUTE                ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan felt the familiar drain. His STAMINA bar—now at 74%—began to slowly tick down. He ignored the fatigue. He had thirty-one hours left.

"Map the revenue from the BGC accounts first," Juan commanded. "Ignore any entries with a 'Pending' tag. They're phantom numbers."

The mouse didn't move, but the folders on his desktop began to open and close with a speed that made the screen flicker. Files were being scanned, data was being extracted, and a master spreadsheet was being built in the background.

*Bip. Bip. Bip.*

His internal **DEADLINE SENSE** chimed. 

*Warning: Elite Mob approaching.*

Juan didn't look up from the screen, but he felt the presence. It wasn't a monster from a portal. It was something far more dangerous in an office environment: a *Chatty Coworker*.

"Heeey, Juan! My man!"

It was Paolo. 

Paolo was a Rank-D Associate from Sales. He was the kind of person who treated the office like a social club and the breakroom like a second home. He was also the personification of a "Time-Waster" debuff.

"I heard the news, bro! Aldrich gave you the Q3? That’s brutal, man. Pure suicide," Paolo said, leaning against Juan's cubicle wall and effectively blocking his light. "Anyway, I was thinking—since you're already doing data stuff, can you just take a quick look at my sales figures for the Laguna branch? It’ll take like five minutes."

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               COMBAT TRIGGERED           ║
║                                          ║
║  Enemy: PAOLO (THE TIME-WASTER)          ║
║  Type: SOCIAL OBSTACLE                   ║
║  Status Effect: [ CHATTY ]               ║
║  (Reduces FOCUS by 40% while active)     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan felt his FOCUS bar plummet. The numbers on the screen began to blur. Rodel’s processing speed slowed down as the System struggled to maintain the connection through the distraction.

"Paolo," Juan said, his voice as sharp as a paper cut. "I have thirty hours to finish a report that Rodel couldn't even finish in a week."

"Yeah, yeah, I know! But Laguna is just—"

"If I finish the Laguna numbers for you," Juan interrupted, finally looking up. His eyes, usually dull and tired, were now cold and focused. "Will you tell Aldrich that you were the one who distracted me when I fail the Q3?"

Paolo blinked. He stepped back. The grin on his face faltered. "Whoa, chill, Juan. It was just a question."

"The answer is no. I’m in a dungeon."

Paolo looked at Juan like he had grown a second head. "A dungeon? You've been working too hard, bro. Seriously. Good luck with your... dungeon."

Paolo retreated, his presence fading.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               COMBAT ENDED               ║
║                                          ║
║  Victory!                                ║
║  Exp Gained: 10                          ║
║  Focus Restored.                         ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan didn't celebrate. He turned back to the screen. 

"Rodel, focus on the Laguna branch data. Paolo just gave me a hint."

*"Hint, boss?"* the shadow whispered.

"If a Sales guy is asking for a 'quick look' at his numbers before a big report, it means he’s hiding a discrepancy. Check the Laguna revenue against the warehouse shipments. There’s a gap there. That’s our first 'Trap'."

The Shadow Rodel dived into the Laguna files. 

Seconds later, a red highlight appeared in the master spreadsheet. 

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               TRAP DISARMED              ║
║                                          ║
║  [ DISCREPANCY DETECTED ]                ║
║  Amount: 4.2 Million PHP                 ║
║  Source: 'Ghost Shipments'               ║
║                                          ║
║  If included, the Q3 report would have   ║
║  failed the 98% accuracy check.          ║
║                                          ║
║  Reward: +100 EXP                        ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan let out a breath he didn't know he was holding. He looked at his watch. 1:15 PM. 

He had survived the first trap. But as he looked at the thousands of rows of data still left to process, he realized that the "Data Void" was much deeper than he had imagined. 

And somewhere at the bottom of it, a real "Boss" error was waiting.
