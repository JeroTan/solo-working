# Chapter 3 — The Shadow Worker

*The most dangerous person in the office isn't the one who shouts. It’s the one who knows exactly how the coffee machine works, where the extra toner is hidden, and now, how to reanimate your professional soul.*

---

*00:00:42...*

The Shadow Rodel didn't walk. It blurred. 

It was a flicker of dark static that slammed into the chair. The keyboard didn't just click; it screamed. Under the shadow’s touch, the mechanical keys sounded like a machine gun. 

Juan watched, mesmerized. In the frozen world of the 11th floor, where a bead of Dennis's sweat was suspended in mid-air and a fly was trapped in a static dance near the ceiling, the Shadow Rodel was the only thing moving.

*00:00:31...*

The monitor on Rodel’s desk was a strobe light of code. Thousands of lines of SQL passed in a heartbeat. The Shadow Rodel wasn't just typing; it was communicating directly with the machine. It bypassed the laggy UI, dove into the backend, and began stitching the corrupted data fields back together with the precision of a digital surgeon.

*00:00:15...*

"Faster," Juan whispered.

His own chest felt tight. A blue bar appeared in his peripheral vision: **[ STAMINA: 64%... 62%... ]**. Using the shadow was literally draining the life out of him. He felt a phantom ache in his fingers, as if he were the one typing at three hundred words per minute.

The shadow’s eyes—two glowing slits of cold, calculated logic—never wavered. It reached the final database cluster. The "CRITICAL ERROR" window flashed one last time, a defiant red block of text.

The Shadow Rodel slammed its hand onto the 'Enter' key.

*00:00:01...*

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║             QUEST CLEAR!                 ║
║                                          ║
║  Objective: Recover 100% data parity.    ║
║  Result: SUCCESS (100.00%)               ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

The timer hit zero.

The static in the air snapped like a broken rubber band. The blue light from the monitors vanished, replaced by the familiar, flickering fluorescent hum of the office. 

Time rushed back in.

"—Rodel! Get back here!" Dennis Soriano’s voice finished the shout that had been frozen in his throat. 

Dennis blinked. He looked at the monitor on the main wall. The red "CRITICAL ERROR" bars were gone. In their place, a green progress bar was racing toward completion.

*98%... 99%... 100%.*

**[ MIGRATION COMPLETE. DATA INTEGRITY VERIFIED. ]**

The room went silent. Not the supernatural silence of the System, but the stunned, heavy silence of fourteen people who had just witnessed a miracle they couldn't explain.

"It... it finished?" Cynthia whispered, her hands still trembling near her face. "How? The AC failed. The servers were melting."

"The temp is back to normal," someone called from the server room door. "The industrial fan? Who put that there?"

Dennis turned, his face pale and sweaty. He looked at Juan. Juan was sitting at his desk, his hands resting on his notebook. He looked as unremarkable as he had two hours ago, except for the slight sheen of sweat on his forehead and the way his shadow seemed just a little bit darker than the lighting should allow.

"Juan," Dennis panted. "Did you... did you do that?"

Juan looked at his manager. He looked at the empty seat where Rodel Magsino used to sit. Beneath that seat, invisible to everyone else, the flickering dark shape of the shadow was curled like a tired dog, waiting.

"I just documented the recovery process, sir," Juan said, his voice as flat as a PDF. "The system must have auto-recovered once the temperature stabilized. Redundancy protocols, probably."

It was a lie. A beautiful, corporate-grade lie. In an office, if you provide a technical-sounding explanation for something good happening, no one asks questions because they’re too busy taking credit for it.

"Right," Dennis said, straightening his tie. "Auto-recovery. Of course. Excellent work, team. We... we did it."

The team didn't cheer. They were too exhausted. They just slumped into their chairs, the adrenaline leaving them in a cold wave.

Juan, however, was staring at a new set of windows that only he could see.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║             TRIAL REWARDS                ║
║                                          ║
║  1. Status: [ SYSTEM WORKER ] UNLOCKED   ║
║  2. Skill: [ RAISE ] PERMANENT           ║
║  3. Shadow Slot: 1/1                     ║
║  4. Stats: +5 FOCUS, +5 DEADLINE SENSE   ║
║                                          ║
║  Current Rank: F                         ║
║  Next Rank at: 1,000 EXP                 ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

He felt it then. A sharp clarity in the back of his mind. The messy, chaotic data of the office suddenly felt like it was being sorted into folders. 

His **DEADLINE SENSE** pulsed. 

*3 hours, 12 minutes until the morning standup.*
*8 hours, 47 minutes until the client's first audit.*

"Juan." 

He looked up. Dennis was standing over him, looking regained. Now that the crisis had passed, the manager's default setting—delegation—had rebooted.

"Rodel walked out. We need someone to finish the technical summary for the 9 AM meeting with VP Lim. You've got the notes, right?"

"I have the notes, sir."

"Good. Finish it by 8:30. I'm going to get some coffee. Or a beer. Maybe both."

Dennis walked away.

Juan looked at the empty workstation of Rodel Magsino. He looked down at his own shadow.

"Raise," he whispered, so low it was barely a breath.

The shadow didn't emerge this time. Instead, a window appeared.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║            SHADOW WORKER INFO            ║
║                                          ║
║  Name: RODEL (THE BROKEN ANALYST)        ║
║  Rank: B (Shadow Form)                   ║
║  Condition: EXTREMELY TIRED              ║
║                                          ║
║  Note: Shadow Workers share your         ║
║  STAMINA pool. Use wisely.               ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan felt a sudden drain in his legs. His STAMINA bar dipped again.

*Focus,* he thought.

He opened the technical summary document. It was a mess. 

"Rodel," Juan murmured. "Format the tables. Highlight the parity successes."

The mouse on his desk didn't move. The keyboard didn't click. But the cursor in the document began to fly. Columns aligned themselves. Formulas appeared in the cells, calculating variances with a speed that would have crashed a normal computer. 

Juan sat back, his hands in his lap. To anyone else, it looked like he was staring blankly at the screen. In reality, he was watching a master at work.

The Shadow Rodel was even better than the real Rodel. It didn't need coffee. It didn't need to complain. It only had the task.

In twelve minutes, the work was done.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              TASK COMPLETE               ║
║                                          ║
║  Exp Gained: 50                          ║
║  Focus Usage: 5%                         ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan saved the document and sent it to Dennis. 

He opened his notebook to a clean page.

*1. Survive the 9 AM meeting.*
*2. Investigate 'Daily Missions'.*
*3. Find out what happens to the real Rodel.*

As he wrote, a small, red notification popped up in the corner of his eye.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              ⚠ HIDDEN QUEST ⚠            ║
║                                          ║
║  [ THE REALITY CHECK ]                   ║
║  A worker has resigned in your presence.  ║
║  The System requires their physical      ║
║  Employee ID badge to bind the shadow.   ║
║                                          ║
║  Objective: Retrieve Rodel's ID badge    ║
║  before Security clears his desk.        ║
║                                          ║
║  TIME LIMIT: 06:00:00                    ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan looked at Rodel's desk. The plastic badge was still lying there, face up.

He stood up. 

He was a Rank-F employee. He was invisible. He was the weakest person in the office.

But as he walked toward the desk, his shadow followed him—not as a dark shape on the floor, but as a silent, flickering partner.

Juan picked up the badge.

"Welcome to the team, Rodel," he whispered.

The badge vanished into his inventory.
