# Chapter 16 — The 24-Hour Sprint

*Time is the only resource that doesn't scale. You can add more developers, more servers, and more coffee, but a day will always have eighty-six thousand four hundred seconds. And for a System Worker, every one of those seconds has a price.*

---

The 11th floor was no longer an office. It was a pressure cooker. 

The clock on the wall read 2:14 AM. Juan dela Cruz was sitting at his desk, his hands trembling as he typed. He had been awake for thirty-eight hours. 

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              STATUS: CRITICAL            ║
║                                          ║
║  STAMINA: 4% [          ]                ║
║  FOCUS: 12% [|         ]                 ║
║                                          ║
║  WARNING: SYSTEM FAILURE IN 18 MINUTES.  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

He wasn't just working. He was holding the world together. 

In his peripheral vision, he could see his two shadows. Rodel was buried in the backend, his dark hands moving so fast they were a blur, fixing the last-minute data corruption from the Apex test environment. Cynthia was in the virtual "Front Room," her violet eyes glowing as she rewrote the help documentation and automated the client’s onboarding emails.

Maintaining both of them was like trying to hold two wild horses with a single thread. 

"Juan," a voice whispered. 

He didn't look up. He couldn't. If he broke his focus for even a second, the connection to the shadows would snap. 

It was Benny. The veteran developer was standing by his cubicle, holding a small, plastic bottle of sports drink and a packet of biscuits.

"You're shaking, kid," Benny said, his voice unusually soft. "Take a break. I’ve got the legacy hooks under control. The API is stable."

"Can't... stop," Juan rasped. "The client... 9 AM."

"The client is a Tier-1 retail conglomerate. They’ve seen projects fail before. They won't die if we’re an hour late."

"But I will."

Juan finally looked at Benny. His eyes were bloodshot, and his skin was the color of old parchment. Benny saw it then—not just the exhaustion, but the *weight*. He saw the way the shadows seemed to be pulling the life out of Juan.

Benny didn't ask questions. He didn't mention the flickering blue windows he couldn't see. He just opened the sports drink and held it to Juan’s lips.

"Drink. Now."

Juan drank. The liquid was cold and sugary, a temporary boost that his System registered as a **[ MINOR RECOVERY ]**.

```
║  STAMINA: 4% → 7%                        ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

"Thanks, Benny," Juan whispered.

"Don't thank me. Just finish the damn thing so I can go home and see my wife."

Benny walked away, back to his bank of monitors. 

Juan closed his eyes and dived back into the data. 

*8 hours left.*

The shadows responded to his renewed focus. The "Phoenix" engine was almost complete. It was a beautiful, terrifying thing—a system that didn't just manage loyalty points, but predicted customer behavior with an accuracy that would make a statistician weep.

But as the sun began to rise over the city, the 'Detection Meter' in his vision flared.

```
║  ALERT: DETECTION METER: 45% [||||     ] ║
║  Isabel Reyes has entered the building.  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

She was early. 

Juan watched the elevator lobby through the glass. Isabel stepped out, her charcoal suit as crisp as if she’d just stepped out of a magazine. She didn't go to her corner. She walked straight to the CEO’s office on the 14th floor. 

The *Aethelgard* audit was over. 

Ten minutes later, the office intercom crackled to life.

"Mr. Juan dela Cruz. Please report to the CEO’s office immediately. Ms. Cynthia Ocampo, you are also requested."

The room went silent. The developers stopped typing. Benny looked at Juan, his face full of dread. 

This wasn't a progress update. This was an interrogation.

Juan stood up. He felt the shadows pull back into his feet, their work unfinished but their presence still strong. He looked at his notebook, the one that contained the truth of everything he had done.

He didn't take it. He left it on his desk, right next to the empty sports drink bottle.

"Cynthia," he said, looking at the lead architect who was already standing by the door. "Are you ready?"

Cynthia nodded, her face pale but her eyes fierce. "I've been ready for this since I was Rank F, Juan."

They walked toward the elevator. As they ascended to the 14th floor, Juan saw a new window appear.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               FINAL DUNGEON              ║
║                                          ║
║  Environment: THE CEO'S OFFICE           ║
║  Boss: GABRIEL SORIANO (THE FINAL ARBITER)║
║  Elite: ISABEL REYES (THE HUNTER)        ║
║                                          ║
║  Stakes: EVERYTHING.                     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

The elevator doors opened. 

The Phoenix had reached the sun. Now it was time to see if it would burn.
