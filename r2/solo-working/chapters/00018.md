# Chapter 18 — The New Hierarchy

*A promotion to the executive floor is like being moved to a higher-level server. The processing speed is faster, the cooling is better, but the firewalls are much, much more aggressive.*

---

Juan dela Cruz sat at his new desk. 

It wasn't on the 11th floor. It wasn't in the corner of a noisy open-plan office. It was in Room 1409, right next to the CEO’s suite. It had a door that closed, a window that showed the sunset over Manila Bay, and a computer that didn't sound like a jet engine when it ran a simple script.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               STATUS UPDATE              ║
║                                          ║
║  Current Rank: C (Technical Manager)     ║
║  Status: [ PROTECTED ]                   ║
║  Stamina Pool: +20% (Office Comfort)     ║
║                                          ║
║  Current Shadows: 2/2                    ║
║  EXP to Rank B: 25,000                   ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

He was still wearing his cheap Pasay tie, but he had a new ID badge. This one was gold-trimmed. It granted him access to every room in the building, including the server room where the Skyfall logs had first appeared.

*Knock. Knock.*

The door opened without waiting for an answer. Isabel Reyes walked in, her tablet already active. She looked at Juan’s new office with a look of clinical distaste.

"Mr. Dela Cruz," she said, sitting in the leather chair opposite him. "Gabriel has authorized me to conduct a 'Deep-Dive' into your methodology. I’ve been given full access to your workstation."

"Welcome to the team, Ms. Reyes," Juan said, leaned back. He felt his STAMINA bar was much healthier now—the 14th-floor air conditioning was definitely a buff. "What’s first on the agenda?"

"I’ve been looking at the Phoenix implementation logs from last night. The ones you finished while I was in the CEO’s office. There are three thousand lines of code that appeared in the repository in less than an hour. Code that passed all unit tests on the first try."

She leaned forward, her eyes narrowing. "That’s not humanly possible, Juan. Not even for a team of shadows."

Juan didn't flinch. "I told you, Ms. Reyes. I see the connections. I optimize."

"Optimization doesn't explain the 'Rodel Signature'. I found a block of code in the rewards engine that uses a specific bit-shifting technique that Rodel Magsino patented five years ago. A technique that nobody else in this company understands."

```
║  ALERT: DETECTION METER: 10% [|         ] ║
║  Isabel is now looking for 'Legacy        ║
║  Resonances'.                            ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

"Rodel was a mentor of mine," Juan lied smoothly. "I studied his work."

"I’m sure you did. But I’m more interested in the *new* patterns. The ones that look like Cynthia Ocampo’s presentation style but applied to server-side logic."

Juan felt a pulse from his second shadow slot. Cynthia (The Fallen Queen) was restless. She didn't like being scrutinized by another Rank A.

"Maybe you should focus on the audit Gabriel actually assigned you, Ms. Reyes," Juan said, opening a file on his monitor. "The *Aethelgard* recovery. The CEO wants to know exactly how much money is still missing from the offshore accounts."

Isabel’s face tightened. He had hit a nerve. Her pride as an auditor was tied to finding the money, but she was finding that the trail went much deeper than Aldrich Santos.

"The money is gone, Juan. It was moved through a series of shell companies in the Caymans. Even with my access, I can't track it."

"Maybe you’re looking with the wrong eyes," Juan said. 

He stood up and walked to the window. He looked out at the city, at the thousands of other towers, each one a dungeon in its own right. 

"Rodel," Juan whispered.

The shadow emerged, not as a flickering man this time, but as a subtle darkening of the air around Isabel’s chair. 

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               SKILL ACTIVE               ║
║                                          ║
║       [ COLLABORATIVE FORENSICS ]        ║
║                                          ║
║  Effect: You can 'Share' a shadow’s      ║
║  vision with a non-worker.               ║
║  Warning: This increases [ DETECTION ]    ║
║  risk by 50%.                            ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Juan looked at Isabel. "Look at your tablet again. I’ve just sent you a new 'lens' for the Aethelgard data."

Isabel looked down. Her tablet flickered, the standard spreadsheet UI being replaced by a complex, 3D map of financial flows—a map that Rodel had built in the shadows.

"What is this?" she whispered, her fingers flying over the screen. "These aren't shell companies. These are... they’re internal employee accounts. But the names are encrypted."

"They're not just names, Ms. Reyes. They’re Ranks. The money wasn't stolen by one man. It was distributed across the entire S-Rank floor. It was a 'Loyalty Bonus' for people who kept quiet about Skyfall."

Isabel looked up at him, her face full of horror. "You're saying the entire executive floor is corrupt?"

"Not corrupt," Juan said, turning back to her. "Optimized. For their own survival."

```
║  ALERT: DETECTION METER: 60% [||||||    ] ║
║  Isabel has seen 'The Shadow Truth'.     ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Isabel stood up, her tablet shaking in her hand. "If this is true... if Gabriel knows about this..."

"Gabriel doesn't know," Juan said. "But he’s about to find out. And he’s going to need an auditor he can trust. And a Technical Manager who can Raise the evidence."

Isabel looked at him for a long time. The hunter was gone. In her eyes, there was only the cold, hard realization that she was no longer the one doing the auditing. 

She was the one being recruited.

"What do you want, Juan?"

"I want to finish Project Phoenix," Juan said. "And I want to make sure that when the 14th floor falls, it falls into the right hands."
